Curry

Description

This is a really basic recipe for chicken curry! For reference, there this recipe won't involve mcaking your own curry blocks or seasoning mixture.
For the sake of simplicity, you'll be using the Gold Jpanese Curry block you can find in plenty of grocery stores!
This recipe can make a large number of meals, and will be sure to fill you, your friends, and your family up! This recipe only calls for one pot - stir fry the veggies and potatoes in a pot, throw in the chicken to fry for a bit, fill the pot to a boil, and some curry blocks, then let simmer for around twenty minutes and you're all good!

Ingredient List

  • Golden Curry Package
  • Your choice of meat (chicken, beef, etc), cubed
  • Onions
  • Potatoes
  • Carrots
  • Salt and Pepper to taste
  • Garlic
  • Ginger

Instructions

  1. Chop your onions, potatoes, and carrots to your desired size (strips for the onions, cubes for the carrots/potatoes is what I prefer!)
  2. Cut your chicken into bite size pieces
  3. Add oil to a large pot and stir fry the onions, roughly 5 to 10 minutes
  4. Add minced garlic and ginger, mixing well
  5. Add the chicken and cook until the outside is browned
  6. Add four cups of water
  7. Add your chicken curry blocks and stir, such that the blocks are fully disolved in the water
  8. Add the potatoes and carrots
  9. Bring the pot to a gentle simmer, then let cook for 15 minutes
